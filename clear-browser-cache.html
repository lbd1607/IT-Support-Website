Clear browser cache and history

If you have noticed your browser acting strangely or not displaying information correctly, you might need to clear your browser’s cache and history. Keeping your browser clean can also keep you safe on the Web, so it’s important to do this regularly. This tutorial will show you how to clear your browser cache and history for Microsoft Edge, Google Chrome, and Mozilla Firefox.

Note: Clearing your browser cache and other data may delete your autofill information, including usernames and passwords. Please be sure to memorize this information or save it using a password manager service before proceeding.

Microsoft Edge

  1. Click the Start menu button at the bottom left corner of your screen. In the search bar, type “edge”. Click on Microsoft Edge.
  2. After Edge opens, locate the Main menu button at the top right hand corner of the browser. Click
  3. After the Main menu pops-up, click Settings at the bottom of the menu.
  4. The Settings menu opens. Halfway down the Settings menu, under Clear browsing data, click Choose what to clear.
  5. The Clear browsing data pane opens. At the top of the pane, ensure that Browsing history, Cookies and saved website data, and Cached data and files are selected. Select any other areas that you would like to clear.
  6. Click on the Clear button just below the selections area.

After the cache has been cleared, an All Clear! message will appear in the Clear browsing data pane. You may close all browser windows and return to the desktop.

Google Chrome

  1. Click the Start menu button at the bottom left corner of your screen. In the search bar, type “chrome”. Click Google Chrome.
  2. After Chrome opens, locate the Main menu button at the top right hand corner of the browser. Click
  3. The Main menu pops-up. Near the bottom of the menu, click Settings.
  4. After the Settings window opens, scroll to the bottom of the window and click Show advanced settings. The Settings window will expand.
  5. Under Privacy, click the Clear browsing data button.
  6. The Clear browsing data dialog box opens. In the drop-down menu next to Obliterate the following items from select the beginning of time.
  7. Ensure that Browsing history, Download history, Cookies and other site and plugin data, and Cached images and files are selected. Selected items will have a checkmark in the box beside them. Select any additional areas that you want to clear.
  8. Click on the Clear browsing data button just below the selections area. A blue circle icon near the bottom of the window will indicate that Chrome is clearing the data.

After your browsing data has been cleared, the Clear browsing data dialog box will disappear. You may close all browser windows and return to the desktop.

Mozilla Firefox

Note: Firefox keeps your browsing cache and history in separate locations. In this section, you will learn how to clear both, starting with the cache and followed by instructions to clear your browsing history in Firefox.

Clear the Cache

  1. Click the Start menu button at the bottom left corner of your screen. In the search bar, type “firefox”. Click Mozilla Firefox.
  2. After Firefox opens, locate the Main menu button at the top right hand corner of the browser. Click
  3. The Main menu pops-up. Near the bottom center of the list, click Options.
  4. The Options tab opens. On the left side of the window near the bottom, click Advanced.
  5. The Advanced tab opens. At the top of the Advanced tab, locate and click Network.
  6. After the Network menu opens, locate the Cached Web Content section. Under the header for this section, you will notice an area that says: “Your web content cache is currently using _ bytes of disk space”.
  7. To the right of the Cached Web Content section, click Clear Now. Your cache has been cleared. Notice that the area under the Cached Web Content section header now reads: “Your web content cache is currently using 0 bytes of disk space”.
  8. Continue following these instructions to learn how to clear your browser history in Firefox or close all browser windows and return to the desktop if you are finished. If you will be continuing from this step, close the Options tab to return to Firefox’s Start Page.

Clear the History

  1. To clear your history in Firefox, locate the Main menu button at the top right hand corner of the browser. Click on the .
  2. After the Main menu pops-up, click History near the middle of the menu.
  3. The History pane pops-up. At the bottom of the pane, click Show All History.
  4. The Library window pops-up. On the left side of the window under History, right-click on Today, then click Delete. Repeat these steps for any other folders under History.

The Library window now shows no entries in History. You may close all browser windows and return to the desktop.
